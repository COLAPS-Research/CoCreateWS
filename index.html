About the workshop

From ad hoc to intentional

Hackathons have become a global phenomenon in higher education — yet they typically run in parallel to formal curricula, guided by experience rather than research. The CoCreate framework changes that, providing structured guidelines for every design decision educators face.

The problem

Existing hackathon guides are domain-specific, experience-based, and focused on engagement rather than learning outcomes. First-time organisers lack practical, research-backed support for educational hackathon design.

The framework

CoCreate covers goal-setting, theme selection, team formation, mentoring, ideation methods, stakeholder involvement, curriculum integration, and assessment — grounded in empirical research across three European pilot institutions.

Mindful TEL

Aligned with ECTEL 2026's theme of intentionality in learning technology, CoCreate moves beyond the assumption that "hackathons are inherently good" towards evidence-based understanding of when, how, and for whom they work.

What you'll take away

A draft hackathon blueprint tailored to your institutional context, familiarity with the CoCreate framework, and membership in a growing community of practice around educational hackathon design.

Workshop programme

Four parts, one blueprint

A carefully structured half-day moving from understanding the framework to applying it in your own context.

P1

Setting the stage

30 minutes

Welcome and introductions, a concise presentation of the CoCreate project and framework through illustrative examples, and a brief individual reflection on your hackathon-related context or interests.

P2

Framework exploration

45 minutes

Facilitated small-group activity analysing a case vignette using the framework's guidelines and decision points. Identify design tensions, trade-offs, and open questions, then share insights in plenary.

P3

Blueprint design activity

75 minutes

Work in small groups (3–5) to draft a hackathon blueprint using the CoCreate framework as scaffold. Address key decision points, document choices and rationale, then present in a gallery walk format for cross-group feedback.

P4

Synthesis & feedback

40 minutes

Facilitated plenary on shared challenges, lessons, and future directions. Complete a structured feedback instrument on the framework. Learn about the CoCreate community of practice and open invitation to stay engaged.

Workshop goals

What we set out to achieve

Five interconnected goals guiding the workshop design and facilitation.

01

Introduce the framework

Share the CoCreate framework and empirical findings from three-institution pilot implementation with the TEL community.

02

Apply to your context

Enable participants to use the framework's guidelines and decision points for their own institutional setting through guided design activities.

03

Critical reflection

Foster reflection on challenges and opportunities of integrating hackathons into formal curricula, including assessment, equity, and scalability.

04

Collect feedback

Gather structured community feedback to directly inform the final CoCreate framework deliverables.

05

Build a network

Create a cross-institutional network of stakeholders interested in educational hackathon design, contributing to the CoCreate community of practice.

06

Publish outcomes

Produce a technical report reflecting workshop outputs, including anonymised blueprint examples and synthesis of participant insights.
